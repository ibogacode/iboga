2. Hoehn & Yahr Staging
Stage

 Stage	 Description	 Functional Impact
 Stage 1	Unilateral involvement only (one side of the body)	Minimal or no functional impairment; tremor, rigidity, or bradykinesia present on one side.
 Stage 1.5	Unilateral and axial involvement (neck or trunk affected).	Slight impact on posture or axial muscles.
 Stage 2	Bilateral involvement without impairment of balance.	Early bilateral symptoms; patient still independent and balanced.
 Stage 2.5	Mild bilateral disease with recovery on pull test.	Slight postural instability, but patient can recover balance independently.
 Stage 3	Mild to moderate bilateral disease; some postural instability; physically independent.	Increased risk of falls; still able to live independently and perform ADLs (Activities of Daily Living).
 Stage 4	Severe disability; still able to walk or stand unassisted.	Marked impairment; may need help with some activities; mobility limited but possible without full assistance.
 Stage 5	Wheelchair-bound or bedridden unless aided.	Fully dependent; unable to stand or walk without assistance.
